Getting started
Accessing the PTO Tab
1
Sign in to the Employee Portal

Go to peachtreerestorations.com/employeeportal and click Sign in with Google. Use your work email address — @peachtreerestorations.com for Peachtree Roofing & Exteriors employees, or @nickgipsonpa.com if you work for NGPA.

Click the PTO tab

In the main navigation row, click PTO. The tab will load your balance dashboard and recent requests automatically.

3
If you don't see the PTO tab

Dashboard overview
Reading Your Balance Dashboard

When you open the PTO tab, the top area shows your current time-off position at a glance.

Making a request
Submitting a PTO Request
1
Click "+ Request Time Off"

In the My PTO panel, click the blue + Request Time Off button. The request form expands below it.

2
Select a leave type

Choose Vacation or Bereavement from the dropdown. Sick days are not submitted through the portal — speak with your manager or HR directly.

3
Set your start and end dates

Pick the first and last dates of your time off. As soon as both dates are filled in, the system calculates and shows the number of business days automatically, excluding weekends and observed holidays.

4
Mark any half days (optional)

If you'll be working a partial day on any day in your range, a Half Days section appears automatically. Check the box next to any day you want as a half day, then choose the shift window — 9am–1pm (morning off) or 1pm–5pm (afternoon off). You can mark multiple days as half days. Each half day counts as 0.5 instead of 1.

5
Add notes (optional)

Use the Notes field for anything relevant — travel context, coverage plans, or a brief reason. This text is visible to your manager in the approval email and card.

6
Click "Submit Request"

The request is saved and a notification email is sent to your manager automatically. You'll see the new request appear in the Recent Requests table with a Pending status.

⚠
2 or more consecutive sick days If your manager manually records two or more consecutive sick days on your behalf, company policy requires a doctor's note. HR will follow up directly.
After you submit
Tracking Your Requests

The Recent Requests table shows all your submissions. Each row shows the type, date range, number of days, and current status.

Pending Approved Declined Cancelled
  • PendingYour request is waiting for manager action. Your balance is not yet reduced.
  • ApprovedYour manager approved the request. Your used-day count is updated and a Google Calendar event is created on the company PTO calendar.
  • DeclinedYour manager declined the request. You receive an email with the reason (if one was given). No balance change occurs.
  • CancelledYou or an admin cancelled the request. If it was approved, the calendar event is removed and your used-day count is reversed.
ℹ
You receive an email notification any time your request moves to Approved, Declined, or Cancelled.

Changing your plans
Cancelling a Request
1
Find the request in Recent Requests

In the My PTO panel, locate the request you want to cancel. A Cancel button appears on the row if the request is Pending or Approved and the start date has not yet passed.

2
Click Cancel and confirm

A confirmation dialog shows the type, dates, and day count. Click Yes, Cancel Request to proceed, or Keep My Request to go back.

3
What happens next

The request status changes to Cancelled. If it was approved: the Google Calendar event is deleted and your used-day count is reduced by the number of days. Both you and your manager receive a cancellation confirmation email.

⚠
You cannot cancel a request whose start date has already passed. If you need to adjust a past-dated request, contact an admin.
Rules to know
Company Policy Summary
  • Advance notice Requests should be submitted at least two weeks in advance. Requests with less notice may be declined unless HR pre-approves an exception.
  • Blackout dates Quarterly all-hands meeting days are blackout dates. Requests covering those days require prior approval from HR before submission.
  • Weekends & holidays Weekend days and observed company holidays are automatically excluded from your day count — you are not charged PTO for them.
  • Rollover cap Up to 5 unused vacation days carry forward to January 1 of the next year. Unused days above that do not roll over.
  • Gifted / Waived days Your manager or HR may add extra days to your vacation bucket as Gifted (a bonus) or Waived Insurance (given in exchange for waiving an insurance option). Both types show in your balance as additional available days.
  • Bereavement W2 employees may request up to 3 bereavement days per year. Submit through the portal using the Bereavement type. 1099 contractors are not eligible for bereavement leave.
Observed holidays — excluded from PTO counts

Getting started
Accessing Team Requests
1
Open the PTO tab and click "Team Requests"

If your account has manager permissions, a second subtab — Team Requests — appears next to My PTO. Click it to see your team's pending and recent requests.

ℹ

Approving requests
Approving a Request

Pending requests from your direct reports appear as cards at the top of the Team Requests panel. Each card shows the employee name, leave type, date range, day count, and any notes they added.

1
Review the request card

Read the type, dates, day count, and notes. Check the Team Overview table below to see if other team members are also out during that period.

2
Click "Approve"

Click the green Approve button on the card. The system will:

  • Update the request status to Approved
  • Create a Google Calendar event on the company PTO calendar, with the employee's email added as an attendee
  • Deduct the approved days from the employee's used-day count
  • Send the employee a confirmation email
3
Watch for a calendar warning

If the company PTO calendar isn't configured, you'll see a yellow warning toast: "Approved, but calendar invite failed." The approval goes through regardless — only the calendar event is affected. Notify an admin if you see this repeatedly.

Declining requests
Declining a Request
1
Click "Decline" on the request card

The portal will prompt you with a text field to enter a reason. This is optional — you may leave it blank.

2
Confirm the decline

Click OK in the prompt. The request status changes to Declined. The employee receives an email with your reason included (if you provided one). No balance change is made.

3
To abort the decline

Click Cancel in the reason prompt. The request stays in Pending status — nothing changes.

⚠
Once declined, the employee will need to submit a new request if they want to re-request different dates. Declined requests cannot be converted to approved directly.

Getting started
Accessing the Manage Balances Tab
1
Open PTO and click "Manage Balances"

Admins see a third subtab labeled Manage Balances. This tab is not visible to regular employees or managers.

2
Select the year

Use the year dropdown at the top of the panel to choose which year's balances you want to work with. The system defaults to the current year.

3
Click "Load"

Click the Load button to pull balances from the PTOBalances Google Sheet. The balance table will populate below.

Understanding what you see
Loading & Reading Balances

The balance table shows one row per employee per year. Each row contains:

  • EmployeeEmail address and name (from UserPermissions)
  • Vac TotalTotal vacation days allocated for the year (editable)
  • Vac UsedVacation days approved and taken (editable)
  • Sick Total / UsedSick day allocation and usage (editable)
  • Brvmt Total / UsedBereavement allocation and usage (editable)
  • Gifted greenExtra vacation days gifted by management — editable directly, or use the + Gift button
  • Waived Ins purpleVacation days awarded in exchange for waiving insurance — editable directly, or use + Gift button
  • Rolled OverUnused vacation days carried over from the prior year — editable directly, or normally set automatically by the year-end rollover process
  • Net Available greenThe employee's real, usable vacation balance — computed live as Vac Total + Gifted + Waived Ins + Rolled Over, minus Vac Used
  • EntitledCalculated from hire date and employee type. If it doesn't match Vac Total, an Apply button appears to update the row.
⚠
Vac Total is not the employee's whole balance It's only the base entitlement for the year — it does not include Gifted days, Waived Insurance days, or Rolled Over days from last year. Those three live in their own columns and only get combined together in the Net Available column. If you're mentally doing "Vac Total minus Vac Used" to judge how many days someone has left, you're looking at an incomplete number — check Net Available instead. This is the same math used for the employee's own "Available" stat on My PTO and the "Days Left" column on the Team Requests roster, so all three should always agree.
✓
The Employee column and the Save / + Gift actions column stay pinned in place while you scroll horizontally, so you can still see whose row you're editing even after scrolling right to reach Rolled Over or Net Available.
Direct edits
Editing a Balance Row
1
Click into any editable field on the row

Vac Total, Vac Used, Sick Total, Sick Used, Brvmt Total, Brvmt Used, Gifted, Waived Ins, and Rolled Over are all directly editable inline — every balance field on the row can be hand-edited here.

2
Make your changes, then click "Save"

Click the Save button on that row. The values are saved immediately. A success toast confirms the write.

3
If Entitled doesn't match Vac Total

An Apply button appears next to the Entitled value. Clicking it sets Vac Total to the calculated entitlement for that employee's tenure and type, then saves the row.

⚠
Direct edits to Used counts should match approved requests in the system. If they drift out of sync, use the Sync All function to recalculate.
ℹ
The Net Available column recalculates live as you type into Vac Total, Vac Used, Gifted, Waived Ins, or Rolled Over — before you even click Save — so you can see the effect of a change immediately.
✓
Backfilling a prior year's rollover manually If an employee's Rolled Over days from last year were never captured by the automated process (e.g., their balance predates it), type the correct number directly into their Rolled Over field and click Save. Once it's seeded this way, the year-end Rollover function (see below) takes over from there going forward — you shouldn't need to hand-edit it again after the first correction.
Adding extra days
Gifting Extra Days / Waived Insurance

The + Gift button on each row opens the Add Extra Days modal. Use this any time you need to add days to an employee's vacation bucket outside of their standard entitlement.

1
Click "+ Gift" on the employee's row

The Add Extra Days modal opens, pre-labeled with the employee's name.

2
Choose the type of extra days

Two radio options appear:

  • Gifted — a bonus day or days from management (e.g., a performance reward or appreciation). Shows as a (+N gifted) tag on the employee's balance bar.
  • Waived Insurance — days given in exchange for the employee opting out of an insurance benefit. Shows as a (+N waived ins) tag on the employee's balance bar.
3
Enter the number of days and confirm

Enter the days to add (you can use 0.5 increments). Click Confirm. The system adds the value on top of any existing gifted or waived days — it does not replace them. The employee's available day count updates immediately.

ℹ
Both Gifted and Waived Insurance days are folded into the Net Available column on Manage Balances, the employee's own Available stat on My PTO, and the rollover calculation at year end. They count exactly like regular vacation days from the employee's perspective — Gifting a day is not the same as raising Vac Total, but the employee can't tell the difference when using it.
Onboarding
Adding an Employee to a Year
1
Click "+ Add Employee"

A prompt asks for the employee's email address. Enter their full @peachtreerestorations.com address and click OK.

2
Designate W2 or 1099

A second prompt asks you to type "W2" or "1099" for this employee. This is saved back into their UserPermissions row (not just used for this one balance), so future syncs, entitlement checks, and rollovers keep using the correct type going forward — you shouldn't need to re-enter it later.

3
The system creates a balance row automatically

Vacation entitlement is calculated from the employee's hire date (as recorded in UserPermissions) combined with the W2/1099 type you just set. W2 employees also get 3 sick days and 3 bereavement days; 1099 employees get neither. Used counts start at 0.

⚠
The employee must already be set up in the system with PTO eligibility enabled before you add them here. If they are missing or marked ineligible, their balance will not be created correctly. Contact the portal administrator if needed. If their email isn't found in UserPermissions at all, you'll get a warning that the W2/1099 designation couldn't be saved there — the balance is still created using what you entered, but fix their UserPermissions row afterward so it stays correct long-term.
Bulk onboarding
Sync All

Sync All is a one-click way to ensure every eligible employee has a balance entry for the selected year. Use it at the start of a new year or any time you've added multiple employees to the system.

1
Select the target year and click "Sync All"

The system reads all PTO-eligible employees and compares them against the selected year's balances.

2
Missing employees are added automatically

Any employee who doesn't yet have a balance row for the selected year gets one created with their correct entitlement. Employees who already have a row are left completely unchanged.

✓
Sync All is safe to run at any time. It never overwrites existing data — it only fills in gaps.
Manual entries
Adding a Manual Record

Use + Add Record when you need to log a PTO event that didn't go through the normal employee submission flow — for example, recording retroactive sick days or a pre-approved verbal request. Note that this creates a request record but does not automatically adjust the balance; adjust the balance separately if needed.

1
Click "+ Add Record" (green border button)

The Add Record modal opens.

2
Fill in the fields
  • Employee — dropdown of all PTO-eligible staff
  • Type — Vacation or Bereavement
  • Start Date / End Date — date range of the event
  • Half day start / Half day end — checkboxes for partial-day events
  • Status — Approved or Pending
  • Notes — any context for the record
3
Click "Add Record"

The record is saved. If the status is Approved, manually update the employee's Vac Used or Sick Used in the balance table to reflect the days, then save.

Year-end process
Running Year-End Rollover

Run rollover in January once all prior-year requests have been finalized. This creates next-year balances for every employee who has a current-year balance but not yet a next-year one.

1
Select the current year and click "Rollover" (purple border button)

A confirmation dialog tells you exactly what will happen. Click OK to proceed.

2
The system calculates unused days for each employee

Formula: vacTotal + vacGifted + vacWaived - vacUsed. The result is capped at 5 days.

3
Next-year rows are created

Each employee gets a new balance entry for the next year with their correct tenure-based entitlement plus their rollover days. Gifted and Waived Insurance start at 0 for the new year. Employees who already have a next-year balance are skipped.

ℹ
Before running rollover — make sure all prior-year requests are finalized (approved or declined) and all balance edits are saved. Any pending requests that haven't been reviewed will skew the unused-day calculation.
